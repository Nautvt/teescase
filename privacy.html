Privacy Policy — TEES Case

Last updated: 21 June 2026

TEES Case (“the app”) is a free, ad-free logic-puzzle game developed by Naut (WorkflowAutomations), based in the Netherlands (“we”, “us”). This policy explains what data the app collects, why, and what your rights are. We have deliberately kept data collection to the minimum needed to run an online leaderboard.

Summary

  • We do not ask for your email, name, phone number, or any account sign-up.
  • We do not show ads and do not use any third-party analytics or advertising/tracking SDKs.
  • We do not sell or share your data with advertisers or data brokers.
  • The only thing shown publicly is the display name you choose and your puzzle completion times, on the in-app leaderboard.
  • The app works fully offline; data only leaves your device to power the leaderboard.

What we collect

When you complete puzzles and the app syncs to our backend, we store:

  • An anonymous device identifier. Generated on your device to link your puzzle results to “you” across sessions and reinstalls. It is not your name, email, or Apple ID, and it is not shared with anyone.
  • A display name (optional). A handle you choose to appear on the leaderboard. You may set, change, or leave it unset at any time in the app’s Settings. If you don’t set one, an anonymous label (e.g. “Player ab12cd”) is shown instead.
  • Gameplay data. For each puzzle you finish: which puzzle (mechanic, size, seed), your completion time, whether hints were used, and the timestamp.

We do not collect: your real name, email address, contacts, location, photos, device sensors, advertising identifiers, or any browsing/usage analytics.

How we use it

  • To compute and display leaderboards and rankings.
  • To attribute your results to your chosen display name and keep your history consistent across reinstalls.
  • To analyse completion times in aggregate across players in order to estimate how difficult each puzzle is, and to build a difficulty-rated catalogue of puzzles. This uses gameplay results in aggregate and is not used to profile you individually.
  • We do not use your data for advertising, and we never sell it.

Where your data is stored

  • On your device. A local database (SQLite) is the primary store, so the app works offline. This data is removed when you delete the app.
  • On our backend. Synced results are stored in our Supabase project (a hosted PostgreSQL service acting as our data processor). Access is restricted by row-level security so a device can only write its own results; leaderboard reads expose only display names and times.

What is shared / public

The display name you choose and your completion times are visible to other players on the in-app leaderboard. That is the only public exposure. The anonymous device identifier is never shown to other users. We never sell your data or share it with third parties for marketing.

Legal basis (GDPR)

As we are based in the EU, the GDPR applies. We process the data above on the basis of our legitimate interest in providing a functional, competitive leaderboard, and on your consent, which you give by choosing a display name and submitting results. You can withdraw at any time (see Your rights).

Data retention

We keep leaderboard results for as long as the app offers a leaderboard. You can request deletion of your data at any time, and deleting the app removes the local copy on your device.

Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access, correct, or delete your data, and to object to or restrict its processing. Because we do not collect any contact details, the way to exercise these rights is to email us with the display name and approximate dates of play so we can locate your records, or simply delete the app to remove the local copy.

To make a request, contact us at the address below.

Children’s privacy

The app is a general-audience puzzle game and is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. The app collects no data beyond the anonymous identifier, optional display name, and gameplay results described above.

Changes to this policy

We may update this policy as the app evolves. Material changes will be reflected by updating the “Last updated” date at the top of this page.
